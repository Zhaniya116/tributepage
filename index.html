The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band, formed in Liverpool in 1960, that comprised John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. They are regarded as the most influential band of all time and were integral to the development of 1960s counterculture and popular music's recognition as an art form. Rooted in skiffle, beat and 1950s rock 'n' roll, their sound incorporated elements of classical music and traditional pop in innovative ways; the band later explored music styles ranging from ballads and Indian music to psychedelia and hard rock. As pioneers in recording, songwriting and artistic presentation, the Beatles revolutionised many aspects of the music industry and were often publicised as leaders of the era's youth and sociocultural movements.

Timeline of their activities:

  • Jun 1940 - Stuart Sutcliffe (original bassist) born
  • Jul 1940 - Richard Starkey (Ringo Starr) born
  • Oct 1940 - John Lennon born
  • Nov 1941 - Pete Best (former drummer) born
  • Jun 1942 - Paul McCartney born
  • Feb 1943 - George Harrison born
  • March 1944 - Pattie Boyd is born
  • June 1945 - John Lennon’s half-sister Victoria is born
  • April 1946 - Jane Asher is born
  • August 1946 - Maureen Cox is born
  • June 1956 - Paul McCartney is given a trumpet for his 14th birthday
  • November 1956 - Paul McCartney watches Lonnie Donegan perform in Liverpool
  • January 1957 - The Cavern Club opens in Mathew Street, Liverpool
  • June 1957 - The Quarrymen enter the TV Star Search competition
  • July 1957 - Live: Rosebery St, Liverpool
  • August 1957 - The Quarrymen first play the Cavern Club
  • October 1957 - Paul McCartney’s debut with the Quarrymen
  • November 1957 - Live: Wilson Hall, Liverpool
  • December 1957 - Live: The Quarrymen at the Cavern Club, Liverpool
  • January 1958 - Live: New Clubmoor Hall, Liverpool
  • February 1958 - George Harrison meets the Quarrymen
  • March 1958 - The Quarrymen play at Paul McCartney’s cousin’s wedding reception
  • May 1958 - John Lennon photographed in Liverpool
  • July 1958 - Recording: In Spite Of All The Danger, That’ll Be The Day
  • December 1958 - Live: wedding reception, Speke, Liverpool
  • January 1959 - Live: Wilson Hall, Liverpool
  • August 1962 - The Beatles replace drummer Pete Best with Richard Starkey, better known as Ringo Starr
  • January 1963 - The Beatles get their first No. 1 single with “Please Please Me.” The band’s debut LP, “Please Please Me,” is released that March
  • December 1965 - The Beatles release “Rubber Soul,” featuring more experimentation, like Harrison’s a sitar on “Norwegian Wood (This Bird Has Flown)”
  • June 1967 - “Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band” is released. The album took hundreds of hours to record and incorporated a 40-piece orchestra for “A Day in the Life”
  • July 1969 - The band begins work on “Abbey Road.” That same month Lennon released his first solo song, “Give Peace a Chance”
  • September 1969 - Lennon tells the rest of the band he’s leaving the group, but agrees not to publicly announce it until after “Abbey Road” is released later in the month
  • March 1970 - Phil Spector begins work on “Let It Be,” remixing, editing and adding overdubs to the material
  • December 1970 - McCartney files to have the Beatles’ contractual partnership dissolved
  • December 1980 - Lennon is shot and killed outside his New York apartment
  • November 2000 - “1,” a compilation of the Beatles’ British and American No. 1 songs, is released
  • November 2001 - Harrison dies of lung cancer
  • November 2003 - “Let it Be … Naked” is released, with McCartney undoing the Phil Spector work he was unhappy with
  • December 2013 - The Beatles release 59 recordings on iTunes, “The Beatles Bootleg Recordings 1963,” featuring material that was about to fall into the public domain without an official release
